Complete Test Information

Everything you need to know about ISSB tests

WAT - Word Association Test

Format: 60 words shown for 15 seconds each. You respond with 1 sentence per word.

Key Points:

  • Always positive, active, and duty-oriented responses
  • Use military/service context when possible
  • Avoid negative words: fear, doubt, fail, weak
  • No one-word answers
  • Speak naturally and confidently
SCT - Sentence Completion Test

Format: 50 sentence stubs per set. 6 sets total = 300 prompts. Complete each naturally in 10 seconds.

Assessment Criteria:

  • Positive, mature, realistic completions
  • Consistency in tone across all 50
  • Avoid contradicting statements
  • Show problem-solving mindset
  • Reflects emotional stability
TAT - Thematic Apperception Test

Format: 12 ambiguous pictures. 30 seconds viewing + 4 minutes writing per story.

Story Structure (CRITICAL):

  1. Situation: Who, where, what is happening
  2. Problem: Challenge or conflict introduced
  3. Action: What protagonist does (shows initiative)
  4. Resolution: Positive, realistic outcome showing growth
SRT - Situation Reaction Test

Format: 55 real-world situations. 30 seconds per response. Shows judgment & maturity.

How to Answer:

  • Practical, immediate action (no hesitation)
  • Take responsibility, avoid blame
  • Show concern for others
  • Realistic solutions, not heroic/theatrical
  • Demonstrates emotional control
GTO - Group Tasks

Evaluated On: Leadership, cooperation, physical fitness, communication, decision-making under pressure.

Key Rule: Cooperation over individual heroics. Help teammates before yourself.
